The Hellenistic Era

1. The start of Hellenism period

The start of the Hellenistic period was origined from the Balkan Campaign in BC 335. During the Campaign, Alexander the Great was able to conquer the Persian Empire and the Achaemenid dynasty and begin the culture exchange between East and West. However, the actual start of the Hellenistic period was two years after the death of Alexander the Great, which was the BC 323.

2. What is Hellenistic culture?

The Hellenistic culture is well known as a time of mixture of Greek and Oriental cultures. After the death of Alexander the Great the large empire was divided into three areas, which are Egypt, Syria, and Macedonia. The Ptolemaic dynasty has ruled the Egypt, the Seleucid dynasty has rule the Syria and lastly the Antigonid dynasty was ruled by Macedonia.
